All you need to know about Cricket

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game game played between two teams of 11 players each. The game follows a set of rules established by the Marylebone Cricket Club (MCC). Here are the key rules:

1. Objective

  • The batting team tries to score as many runs as possible, while the bowling/fielding team tries to dismiss the batters and limit the runs.
  • The team that scores more runs wins the match.

2. Formats of Cricket

  • Test Cricket (5-day matches, two innings per team)
  • One Day International (ODI) (50 overs per team)
  • T20 (Twenty20) (20 overs per team)

3. Basic Gameplay

  • Each team has 11 players: batters, bowlers, and fielders.
  • The game is played on a circular/oval field with a 22-yard pitch in the center.
  • One team bats, and the other team fields and bowls.

4. Results of the Match

  • Win : The team with more runs wins.
  • Tie : Both teams score the same runs (Super Over in T20s & ODIs).
  • Draw : Test matches can end without a result if time runs out.
